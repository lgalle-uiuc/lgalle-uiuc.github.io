Fuel efficiency in gasoline vehicles is typically lower compared to diesel and electric vehicles. Gasoline engines operate by igniting a mixture of fuel and air, which generates power through a series of controlled explosions. This process tends to be less efficient because a significant amount of energy is lost as heat. On average, gasoline vehicles achieve about 25-30 miles per gallon (MPG) for standard cars, though this can vary widely based on the make and model.
